Producer Collective

Your Official User Guide

1. Introduction: What is Producer Collective?

Welcome to Producer Collective, an AI-powered music creation tool that lets you craft unique beats in real-time. By blending the signature styles of legendary producers and a wide array of instruments, you can explore new sonic territories and find inspiration instantly. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to become a power user.

2. The Main Interface & Controls

The app is divided into a few key areas designed for intuitive navigation and control.

The Producer Grid (Overview Page)

This is your home base. It displays all available producers and instruments. A highlighted tab indicates that the producer is active in your current mix.

Header Controls

  • Play/Pause: The large central button starts and stops the music.
  • Global Modifiers (Mood, Key, Scale): These dropdowns instantly change the emotional character, key, and scale of your entire track.
  • BPM Display: Shows the real-time Beats Per Minute of the generated music. This is crucial for syncing with other software.
  • Top-Left Buttons:
    • ?: Opens this user guide.
    • MIDI: Opens settings to connect a MIDI controller.
    • ALL OFF: A handy reset button that turns all knobs down to zero.
    • AI Prompt: Takes you to a page where you can control the board with text.
    • Styles: Opens a menu of pre-made templates to get you started quickly.
    • Macros: A view for controlling all master producer volumes at once, with an AI text prompt for quick adjustments.

3. Crafting Your Sound

Click on any producer or instrument from the grid to enter the "Detail Page" where you can shape their sound.

Producer Pods

Standard producers have a master knob and three smaller "ingredient" knobs.

  • Master Knob: Controls the overall volume and presence of that producer in the mix.
  • Ingredient Knobs: Fine-tune the specific elements of that producer's signature style (e.g., 'G-Funk synth lead' for Dr. Dre).
  • Spice Buttons: Many producers have one or two 'Spice' buttons to add a signature flourish or change their style.
  • Solo Drums Button: Instantly isolates the drum and percussion elements from that producer's style.

Instrument Pods & Style Selectors

Instruments (like Violin, Cello, or the Drummer) have a master knob and one or more dropdown style selectors.

  • Master Knob: Controls the volume of the instrument.
  • Style Selectors: These dropdowns let you choose the performance style. For example, you can make the Violin play in a 'Cinematic' or 'Pop' style.

4. AI-Powered Creativity

Go beyond knobs with powerful AI features that write the music for you.

The Styles Menu

Click the green Styles button to access a list of curated musical templates. Clicking one will automatically set all the knobs and selectors to create that sound, giving you a perfect starting point.

The AI Prompt Page

Click the purple AI Prompt button to open a text box. Here, you can describe the music you want to create in your own words. The AI will interpret your request and adjust the entire mixing board to match your vision. For example:

Create a funky 70s disco track with a soulful bassline and an epic brass section.

The Macros Page

Click the blue Macros button to see all producer master volumes at once. You can use the text prompt here for simpler commands focused on mixing, like:

Turn up Dr. Dre and turn down Timbaland a bit.

5. Syncing with a DAW (Example: Logic Pro)

Integrate Producer Collective into your professional workflow by syncing it with your Digital Audio Workstation (DAW).

Prerequisite: To route audio from your browser to a DAW on macOS, you need a free virtual audio driver. We highly recommend BlackHole, available from existential.audio.

Step-by-Step Audio Routing

  1. Install BlackHole.
  2. Create a Multi-Output Device: Open the "Audio MIDI Setup" app on your Mac. Click the + icon and select "Create Multi-Output Device." Check the boxes for both your normal output (e.g., headphones) and "BlackHole."
  3. Set System Output: Right-click your new Multi-Output Device and select "Use This Device For Sound Output."
  4. Configure Logic Pro: In Logic's audio settings (Logic Pro > Settings > Audio), set your Input Device to "BlackHole."
  5. Record: Create a new Audio Track in Logic and set its input to Input 1-2. When you play music in the Producer Collective app, you can now record it directly into Logic.

Transport & Tempo Sync

Transport (Play/Stop): The app listens for MIDI transport commands from your DAW. When you press Play in Logic, the app will start. The "DAW SYNC ACTIVE" indicator will appear to confirm the connection.

Tempo (BPM): This is a manual process. Play music in the app and note the BPM in the header. Manually type this number into your Logic Pro project's tempo field. Now your DAW's grid will be perfectly aligned with the music.

6. Troubleshooting & Tips

  • Music won't play or stops suddenly: The app requires at least one knob to be turned up. If you see the error "Turn up a knob to start," simply adjust any knob. The "ALL OFF" button is a quick way to reset if you get lost.
  • Filtered Prompts (Red Highlight): Occasionally, the AI might filter a prompt if it's too instructional. When this happens, the knob's text will turn red. Try a different prompt or adjust your mix.
  • Creative Tip: The magic happens when you blend unexpected styles! Try combining a hip-hop producer with a cinematic string section, then use the "Mood" selector to instantly change the vibe from 'Epic' to 'Melancholy' to spark new ideas.
